Incidence Rate

TPD -> incidence rate and SI is fixed -> GLM model similar factors given in mortality

For DII,

·         Replacement ratio – the ratio insured benefits to pre-disablement earnings. Benefit as an income stream replaced the original income.

·         Agreed value or indemnity – agreed value gives a fixed amount of benefit, indemnity is a percentage, so the replacement ratio is fixed.

·         Waiting or deferment period – longer waiting period will be a lower incidence rate

·         Benefit period – life time vs 2 years.

DII claim termination – actual claims terminated at each duration vs expected. The DII pays until

Recovery, dies, commutes to lump sum, end of the benefit period. Interested in how long, this would like a survival model

Trauma the definition is more well defined. Incidence rate is increasing given the better diagnosis testing (speed testing). The occupation is generally less used.

IBNR/RBNA are significant for morbidity claims, e.g. waiting period, DII, leaving a tail for the liability. For more significant IBNR, chain ladder should be used.

Setting assumptions

Trends in disability experience

·         Definition of disability – is changing, not meeting own occupation->any occupation. Terms like reasonably, likely.

·         Interpretation of disablement -> this can be different from one to one, or change in the culture

·         Medical criteria -> not accepting mental health

·         Underwriting standards, changes by moving to automatic underwriting or acceptance

·         Replacement ratio -> if high, more incentive not returning to work

·         Economic -> when unemployment rate is high, harder to return to work

·         Group business -> employees are more aware of the benefits

·         Law firms to help fight to the claims

·         Claim mangers: workload, styles, skills

Trends in trauma experience

·         The disease can be diagnosed in early stage, speed testing

·         Trend are very country specific so we cannot rely on other oversea market

·         Obesity may make the trend worse.
