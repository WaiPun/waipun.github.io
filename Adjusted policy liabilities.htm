Lump Sum (TPD, Trauma, Term)

Stepped premium

Day one, the APL and PL are equal, because:

·         The PVFPM is set up for offset the negative BEL to avoid the day one gain.

·         The APL is floored by termination value which is 0 (typically)

 

The BEL will still be negative all the time and the PVFPM will release over time. The BEL is typically more negative because acquisition cost and commission are not included in the BEL. The PL is typically negative.

The APL will be still floored by claim reserve or unearned premium reserve.

Level premium

The day one is same as the stepped and the early stage is similar to the stepped premium (APL is greater).

In mid to late stage of the policy, the BEL will be positive and APL won’t be subject to the termination value flooring.

Under both bases, the cash flow are discounted by risk free rate. The PL will be greater because PL = RFBEL + PVFPM.

Disability income

Active lives

Same as the Lump sum

Disability lives

They are basically the same. When turning disabled, the change in reserve from (Incidence rate * PV(disability benefit) to disabled reserve will fully consume the PVFRM.

PVFPM should be zero if the insured is disability, under the profit margin is very heavy.

Annuity

Immediate annuity

No surrender value, so the termination values won’t bite in this case.

Under LAGIC, the annuity is allowed to discount with risk free rate plus an illiquidity premium. The APL will be small.

With saving element

Including Par, Investment account

 

For day 1, they are equal.

Early stage, still APL is greater.

In the later stage, it is inconclusive as it depends on the risk premium in the discount used in the PL and the PVFPM.

Group business

Accumulation method

PL is greater.

Assume an adequacy test was conducted to demonstrated that the UPR + claim reserve – DAC is more conservative than RFBEL. The termination value for group business is 0 under LPS 360. Hence the PL is greater than the APL.
